Strategic Planning

In 2017, DORIS undertook an agency-wide strategic planning process that engaged every employee during a four month period. DORIS engaged the services of Public School – a not-for-profit founded to help local governments design and deliver better public services. There were two phases: one-on-one meetings with a consultant and every DORIS employee who was interested in speaking with him (over 60% of staff) to gather opinions about what worked/didn't work at the agency. Staff-identified needs were incorporated into discussions of the strategic planning group – a mix of senior and frontline staff members who had been with the agency for both short and long periods of time. The group set eight strategic priorities. Two of the eight strategic planning priorities evolved directly from concerns raised in the staff interviews. We established participatory committees to set goals and deliverables for each of the priorities. The Committees meet monthly and their minutes are posted on the Intranet. DORIS has begun to implement activities that will achieve the following eight priorities:

  • Develop open, trustworthy communications in all parts of the agency
  • Build a sense of community and create opportunities for staff to collaborate across teams
  • Develop and empower Human Capital
  • Increase agency oversight, accountability, and transparency for all operations
  • Provide quality customer service for all operations
  • Expand outreach and access to all collections to a diverse range of customers, stakeholders and patrons
  • Modernize current operations and facilities, and execute on innovation through digital assets
  • Improve physical and intellectual control of records
